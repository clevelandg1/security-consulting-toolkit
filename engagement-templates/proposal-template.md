## Methodology

This engagement follows the **Customer Advisory Framework (CAF)**, a structured approach to delivering continuous, measurable security value. The CAF consists of four repeating phases:

### Phase 1: Baseline Assessment

*Objective: Understand where the client is today.*

- Conduct a structured kickoff meeting using the CAF meeting structure
- Complete all pre-engagement discovery questions (see Section 5)
- Document current state across vulnerability management, SIEM coverage, asset inventory, cloud environments, and incident response
- Identify immediate risk areas and quick-win opportunities
- Produce a Baseline Security Posture Report

### Phase 2: Discovery & Gap Analysis

*Objective: Identify gaps between current state and target state.*

- Map findings against the applicable security framework (NIST CSF, CIS Controls, SOC 2 TSC, etc.)
- Prioritize gaps by risk severity and remediation effort
- Develop a Gap Remediation Roadmap with recommended actions, owners, and timelines
- Align gaps to business risk using client-provided asset criticality data

### Phase 3: Ongoing Advisory

*Objective: Provide regular expert guidance and hold the client accountable to progress.*

- Monthly advisory meetings following the CAF meeting structure:
  1. Review Purpose & Goals
  2. Current Posture Overview
  3. Discovery / Check-In Questions
  4. Progress Review
  5. Gap Identification
  6. Improvements & Recommendations
  7. Action Items & Next Steps
- Track remediation progress against SLAs
- Introduce targeted improvements each session (e.g., new log sources, detection rules, IR process enhancements)
- Maintain a living action item log

### Phase 4: Quarterly Reviews

*Objective: Deliver executive-level reporting on posture improvements and set the agenda for the next quarter.*

- Quarterly Business Review (QBR) with metrics-based progress reporting
- Executive summary suitable for CISO, CTO, or board-level consumption
- Posture trend analysis (quarter over quarter)
- Identification of new initiatives, compliance deadlines, or business changes that affect security priorities
- Renewal and expansion planning as appropriate

## Pre-Engagement Discovery Questions

The following questions will be reviewed during the kickoff meeting and may be submitted in advance to accelerate the baseline assessment. Answers will be treated as confidential.

### Security Goals & Compliance

- What are your top three security priorities for the next 12 months?
- Are you subject to any regulatory or compliance frameworks (e.g., SOC 2, HIPAA, PCI-DSS, NIST, ISO 27001, CMMC)?
- Do you have any upcoming audits, assessments, or certification renewals?
- How is security investment currently justified to leadership? Are there specific metrics leadership tracks?

### Current Tools & Technology

- What security tools are currently deployed in your environment? *(e.g., EDR, SIEM, vulnerability scanner, firewall, email security, identity management)*
- Are any tools underutilized or not producing actionable output?
- What is your current SIEM or log management platform? How long has it been in place?
- Do you have a security dashboard or reporting mechanism today?

### Vulnerability Management

- Do you have a formal vulnerability management program? If so, what tools are used for scanning?
- How frequently do you scan endpoints, servers, and network devices?
- Do you currently have defined SLAs for remediating vulnerabilities by severity (critical, high, medium, low)?
- What does your current vulnerability backlog look like? Do you track age of open vulnerabilities?
- Who owns remediation — security, IT, or a shared responsibility?

### Asset Inventory & Criticality

- Do you maintain a current asset inventory? What tool or system is used?
- How do you classify asset criticality (e.g., by business function, data type, regulatory relevance)?
- Do you have visibility into cloud-hosted assets in addition to on-premises infrastructure?
- Are there known gaps in your asset inventory (e.g., IoT devices, contractor systems, shadow IT)?

### Log Collection & SIEM

- What log sources are currently feeding into your SIEM or log aggregation platform?
- Do you have coverage across endpoints, servers, network devices, identity providers (Active Directory / Azure AD / Okta), and cloud environments?
- Are there known log source gaps you have already identified?
- How are SIEM alerts reviewed and triaged today? Is this done internally or by an MSSP?

### Incident Response & SLAs

- Do you have a documented incident response (IR) plan?
- When was the IR plan last tested (tabletop exercise or live incident)?
- Do you have defined response SLAs for different incident severities?
- Have you experienced a significant security incident in the past 12-24 months? What was the impact and outcome?
- Who is responsible for IR coordination — an internal team, an MSSP, or a combination?

### External Exposure

- Do you have visibility into your internet-exposed assets and services?
- Do you conduct regular external vulnerability assessments or use an external attack surface management (EASM) tool?
- Are there known external-facing applications, APIs, or services that receive limited security attention?
