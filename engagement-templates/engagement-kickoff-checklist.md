- [ ] Send meeting invitation with agenda at least [5] business days in advance

- [ ] Confirm Client-side attendees (names, titles, roles)
- [ ] Prepare baseline discovery question list (see Section 4)
- [ ] Research Client's industry, known compliance requirements, and recent public security news

- [ ] Prepare engagement overview slides or briefing document
- [ ] Set up note-taking document with agenda sections pre-populated
- [ ] Confirm screen-sharing and recording capabilities (if recording is authorized)

## 2. Meeting Opening — Review Purpose

*Time allocation: 10 minutes*

Use this section to open the meeting, establish context, and set expectations.

**Facilitator Talking Points:**

- [ ] Welcome attendees and thank Client for their time and engagement
- [ ] Introduce [Firm Name] team members — name, role, relevant background
  - *e.g., "[Name] will be your primary advisory contact. [Name] will support on the technical analysis side."*
- [ ] Request Client team member introductions — name, title, primary responsibilities relevant to security
- [ ] State the purpose of today's meeting:
  - *"The goal of today's kickoff is to establish a shared understanding of where [Client Name] stands today, learn about your priorities and environment, and align on what success looks like for this engagement."*
- [ ] Briefly describe the engagement model:
  - Baseline assessment → ongoing monthly advisory meetings → quarterly business reviews → continuous gap tracking
- [ ] Confirm engagement scope at a high level and invite any immediate clarifying questions
- [ ] Set expectations for what today's meeting will and will not cover
- [ ] Confirm meeting logistics: duration, note-taking, follow-up process
